Connecting your social media accounts to AI TwinBrain lets you publish and schedule content directly from the platform, no copy-pasting, no manual posting. AI TwinBrain supports LinkedIn, Twitter/X, Instagram, Facebook, and Bluesky.

## Supported platforms

- LinkedIn
- Twitter/X
- Instagram
- Facebook
- Bluesky

## Connect an account

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Settings">
    Navigate to **Settings** in your AI TwinBrain dashboard.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open Integrations">
    Click **Integrations** to see all available platforms.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select a platform">
    Click on the platform you want to connect.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Authorize AI TwinBrain">
    You'll be redirected to that platform's authorization page. Log in and grant AI TwinBrain access.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Return to AI TwinBrain">
    After authorizing, you'll be redirected back to AI TwinBrain. Your account is now connected and ready to publish to.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Connecting Bluesky

Bluesky requires one extra step before the redirect. When you click the Bluesky option, enter your Bluesky handle (nickname) first, then complete the authorization on the Bluesky page.

## Account limits

Each workspace supports one connected account per platform. If you manage multiple brands or need separate accounts, create additional workspaces, each additional workspace receives a 40% discount.

## Connected channels by plan

| Plan | Connected channels |
|---|---|
| Starter | 2 channels |
| Pro | 4 channels |
| Leader | Unlimited |

<Note>
  AI TwinBrain supports LinkedIn posts but not LinkedIn articles, LinkedIn's API does not allow publishing articles. You can still publish standard LinkedIn posts.
</Note>

## LinkedIn personal vs. business page

Each workspace supports one LinkedIn connection, either your personal profile or your company page. If you need to publish to both, the workaround is to connect your business page through the Super Agent connectors instead. Tell your agent which connection to use for publishing so it knows to route content to the right destination.

<Tip>
  Add links in comments rather than in the post body. Links in posts often reduce reach due to platform algorithms.
</Tip>
